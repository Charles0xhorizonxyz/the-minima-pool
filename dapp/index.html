Where the fee goes

The 1% swap fee stays inside the pool reserves. There is no fee wallet, treasury, or manual claim address.

All swap fees are handed to liquidity providers through the pool itself: the fees remain in reserves and increase the value behind LP tokens. This compensates LPs for locking their assets, making them available to traders, and bearing price movement and impermanent loss risk.

Impermanent loss warning, please read before investing

When you provide liquidity, your position is continuously rebalanced between MINIMA and USDT. If MINIMA moves strongly, you may end up with less value than if you had simply held both tokens outside the pool.

If MINIMA price drops 50% ~5.72% less than holding
If MINIMA price drops 80% ~25.46% less than holding
If MINIMA price doubles ~5.72% less than holding
If MINIMA price moves 5x ~25.46% less than holding

The goal of providing liquidity is for earned swap fees to become larger than impermanent loss, volatility risk, and the opportunity cost of not simply holding the tokens outside the pool. Providing liquidity for a short period has a higher probability of ending in a loss, because fees may not have enough time to offset losses caused by price movement.

The best scenario for a liquidity provider is high swap volume, meaningful fee generation, and a relatively stable asset price over a long period. The worst scenario is high asset volatility with light swap volume, where impermanent loss can exceed the income earned from user transaction fees.

Read about impermanent loss before adding liquidity. Asking an AI to explain it with your own numbers can help, and this article is a useful starting point: What I Wish Someone Had Told Me About Impermanent Loss Before I Experienced It.

LP holders vote on the swap fee once per quarter. The vote stays open for 72 hours, and LP tokens used to vote are frozen until the voting period ends. The fee can only move by 0.25% per vote, up or down. Voting power is based on pool percentage held and weighted by how long the LP position has been active.
